# ToCProject
Tables of Contents for rare Lisp books

The purpose of this project is to document the tables of contents for various hard-to-find and out of print computer books, mostly about the Lisp programming language. This grew out of my personal frustration when searching for copies of these difficult to find (and often expensive) volumes: before purchasing or dedicating the time to finding a copy, I simply wanted to have an idea of the information these books contained. Naturally, the most straightforward way to get a high-level overview of that would be to view the table of contents. However, many of these books have been out of print for a considerable amount of time---often decades---and it is nigh impossible to find an overview of their contents.

My hope is that, with this project, others can get a glimpse into these volumes and then decide for themselves if they want to track down copies of their own.

I have intentionally limited the list to books that I would consider to be "informational rare"; limited posts in forums, blogs, reviews, or retail websites about them or their contents. Books that are widely available online either for purchase through retailers that provide previews or free downloads have intentionally not been included here. If there are any I've missed (and that must assuredly be the case), please feel free to request them; I'll do my best to track the information down and include it here in a future update.

Disclaimer: The only information contained here is that which appears in the table of contents; I include no further material and what is shown is solely for informational purposes.
