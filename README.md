# ToCProject

## Tables of Contents for rare computing books<br> (mostly about Lisp)

### Purpose
The purpose of this project is to document the tables of contents for various hard-to-find and out of print computing books, predominantly about the Lisp programming language. This grew out of my personal frustration when searching for copies of these difficult to find volumes: before purchasing or even dedicating the time to find a copy, I simply wanted to have an idea of the information these books contained. Naturally, the most straightforward way to get a high-level overview would be to view the table of contents. However, many of these books have been out of print for a considerable amount of time — often decades — and it is nearly impossible to find an overview of their contents.

My hope is that, with this project, others can get a glimpse into these volumes and be able to decide for themselves if they want to track down copies of their own. The collection here is drawn from my personal library. I've collected these books over the course of many years and hope that this can help preserve some of the long and varied publishing history surrounding this language.

### A Note on the Selection
I have intentionally limited the list to books that I would consider "informationally rare"; i.e., those with little or no presence online in forums, blogs, message boards, reviews, or retail websites. Books that are widely available online (new or used) either for free or via purchase through retailers that provide previews have _not_ been included here. If there are any books I've missed — and that must surely be the case given the extensive lifetime of Lisp — please feel free to request them; I'll do my best to track down the information and include it here in a future update.

### A Note on the Style
Each ToC is included as a PDF generated from LaTeX, written by myself. While the style of each of these books is unique (from font selection, layout, numbering, capitalization, etc.), I have attempted to stay uniform in my own layout and font choices. My goal in this is to convey the information in a consistent manner, rather than attempt to capture the individual characteristics of each book. I leave the books, and their authors, to speak for themselves and encourage anyone interested in experiencing these pieces of computing history to find copies of their own.

### Disclaimer
The only information contained here is that which appears in the table of contents; I include no further published material, and what is shown is solely for informational purposes. In the README that accompanies each ToC I include the title, all authors, the year of publication, publisher, and ISBN.
